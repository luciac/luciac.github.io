1950's trivia game

a nation unprepared

Down in Fredricksburg, Texas the National Museum of the Pacific War was creating a new interactive station that was a trivia game about life in general during the late 1940's and early 1950's. Staring at printed advertisements, military brochures, game board packages, and the printing technology of the time, I arrived at simple pallet of colours, typefaces, and visual elements that best represented its time. Most board games used very few colours in their box packaging. Several printed materials at the time used the primary colours heavily. And products targeted towards children used illustrations over photography. All of my research of the 40's and 50's informed the final design of the trivia game.

the printed age made digital

Board games and game shows fused together to inspire the look and feel of 'A Nation Unprepared'. The intro screen was made to immitate the box of a board game. The "brand new" badge at the top right corner gives it an extra little touch. The animations of the correct and incorrect answers were inspired directly from watching old game shows, with the analogue technology of the time. They flipped cards, spun boxes, slid papers, lit up boxes. Every part of this trivia game was carefully inspired by endless research.
