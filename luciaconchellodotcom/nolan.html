Christopher Nolan dvd box set

Christopher Nolan is one of the greatest directors of all times in my own opinion. One of the aspects of his films that stands out most to me is his sense of layering. Layering is the underlying concept behind the entire box set from the box, to the catalogue. His films are reminiscent of film noir and aren’t needlessly decorative. This is why the box was designed black and simple. The box contains three of his most layered films, at the time this set was made, 2016.

   Memento (2000)
   Inception (2010)
   Interstellar (2014)

Nolan is a man of old and practical tastes. He prefers the physical over the digital. He always films on film rather than digital. If he can build a set rather than do it in CG, he will absolutely do it, even if it seems impossible or more difficult. He is a man who appreciates the real and tactile. So a hand crafted physically interactive DVD box set and catalogue was the best way to pay homage to the cinematic genius who brought the world so many amazing films.

The catalogue inclueded several fold outs, creating multiple layers of information, much like his films.

The design of many of these fold outs and special effects are directly inspired by the movies themselves. In the case of the fold out above, it is inspired by the way time is physically stretched in Interstellar.

One of my favourite films of all times, Memento, is told in black and white clips interlaced with color until they blend together. Again, the mechanics of the film were translated into the medium of print to better capture his art within the catalogue.

The closing spread of the book, talks about his use of multiple layers throughout his films. And so this final spread breaks its content into multiple layers.


It is really quite difficult to really recreate the experience of openening and exploring the catalogue digitally. The experience would be entirely different. The gifs are there to show you the design. But if you're a film or Christopher Nolan fanatic as I am, and/or are curious to read the catalogue, read it here! But when you do, remember, I am a designer, not a writer. :)
