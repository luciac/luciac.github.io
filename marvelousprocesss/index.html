MAKING THE MARVEL CINEMATIC UNIVERSE INTERFACE

In the Beginning

Coming up with the idea

When Captain America 2: Winter Soldier came out, I took my mother to see it. She has no idea what Marvel even is. She has never heard of Captain America. So for the entirety of the film, I gave my mother buckets of back story. Most of the characters come from previous films, the main antagonist of the film is from the first Captain America. The "good guys" and "bad guys" are also from Captain America 1. There were references to things that were going to happen in future films. There was a lot to think about. So it dawned on me that there wasn't anything on the internet that collected this mass of data into a helpful and simultaneously kick ass website for people to just explore and understand the bits and pieces.

Research

Information + Inspiration

It was important to start collecting the facts that make up this magnificent world. As well as find out how other cinematic worlds handle this issue of representing their information. So I did three things. I scoured the internet for awesome websites, started watching all of the films over again, and made my "marvel sketchbook" to collect all of my findings. Starting from the front I collected data. Starting from the back I sketched inspiration. One day they will meet in the middle to form the final final product.

Churning out the designs

Organizing Characters

The first portion I tried to tackle was the characters. Initial reaction was to put all of the characters in one list but then I realized that in a 24 film/8 tv show franchise the character list would be ridiculous. So then I tried other methods. By franchise? But there were way to many franchises too. Heros + Villains? And then I realised what about Thor's girl friend? She's not a hero. So then Civilians came to being. And then I thought about Nick Fury. What did he fit into. He was more special than a civilian. So then I added S.H.I.E.L.D. Agents to the mix. And bam there were four categories.

Ideas

Feedback

I was told it needed more connectivity that everything felt very separate. Lightbulbs went off in my head and and I got to designing.

Full Page

The idea of letting the user see the full website upon landing hit me. It gave the idea of depth and multitude of information. I thought it would zoom in when you land, into the film profile page and that from there you could navigate through the drop down menu. I also was inspired by a website I found that used hovering on edges to pull in the next page. So I thought what if the edges were the differnt pages of the website. and hovering on the edges pulled in the different pages.

Site Map

Feedback

A friend of mine asked me to think for a moment what the lay out of using the edge navication would look like in real life as a 3 dimensional object. How would it function. If I went from page A>B what would replace A on the edges? So we thought long and hard about how this organization worked in 3 dimensions. The Cube was the answer.
